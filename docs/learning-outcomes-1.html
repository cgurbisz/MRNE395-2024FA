Learning outcomes

Week Date Learning outcomes Resources Assessment Activity
1 30-Aug
  1. Explain the linkages between the Clean Water Act, the Chesapeake Watershed Agreement Goals and Outcomes, and environmental monitoring
  2. List basic water quality parameters
  3. Describe the physical/biological/chemical states/rates captured by water quality measurements and how they relate particular Goals and Outcomes
  4. Discuss whether we are on track to meeting particular Goals and Outcomes and how we know whether we are on track
  • Chesapeake Progress
  • Tango & Batiuk 2013 Deriving Chesapeake Bay water quality standards
Basic water quality measurements on dock: bring weather protection
2 06-Sep
  1. Explain how attainment criteria for xx parameters were derived
  1. Explain how a vertical water profile is collected
  2. Explain why some water quality parameters may change with depth through the water column
  3. Explain why vertical water profiles are necessary to track attainment of the dissolved oxygen criteria
  4. Define quality assurance (QA) and quality control (QC) and explain the role of QA/QC plans in environmental monitoring
  5. Filter water for chl a and TSS analysis using proper methods
  6. Explain how concentrations are derived from chl a and TSS filtrate; describe the loss on ignition (LOI) method for measuring organic solids
  7. Follow proper procedures to filter water for chl a and TSS using a vacuum pump
